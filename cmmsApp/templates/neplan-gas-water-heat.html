Overview

NEPLAN GAS, WATER & HEATING/COOLING - modular software solution for planning, simulation, optimisation & operation.

NEPLAN Gas, Water & Heating / Cooling is the modular software solution for planning, simulation, optimisation and operation of non-electrical infrastructure networks. It is designed for network types such as gas pipelines, water supply and distribution, heating and cooling systems, as well as district heating/cooling. With its intuitive graphical user interface, NEPLAN enables engineers, operators, utilities and planners to model, design, analyse and optimise these networks with precision and efficiency.

One of NEPLAN’s distinguishing capabilities is its support for integrated energy networks it is the only tool on the market that allows simultaneous simulation of electricity, gas, water and heating networks. This lets users study the interdependencies and interactions between these networks (for example, how gas-fueled heating or combined heat and power feeds into electricity, or how water cooling/heating loads affect electrical and thermal systems) in a coherent, mutual-feedback environment.

  • Integrated Multi-Network Simulation

    As noted, ability to model electricity, gas, water, and heating/cooling networks together. Great for planning in smart cities, multi-energy systems, district thermal networks.

  • Modular & Customisable

    You pick the modules you need (gas, water, heating/cooling) with modular add-ons such as time simulations, fire-water planning, etc.

  • Detailed Thermo-hydraulic Modelling

    Full modelling of flows, pressures, temperature dependencies, pump & valve behaviour, pressure losses (bends, junctions etc.), compressible/incompressible gas, etc.

  • Integration & Data Interoperability:

    GIS and map support; import/export; shared databases; web services / APIs; ability to update library data; client-server / multi-user / SQL database support.

  • Large-Scale Dynamic Network Simulation

    Perform detailed, time-based simulations on very large networks to analyze their behavior over daily, weekly, or seasonal periods under varying demands and supply conditions.

Key Modules & Features

Principal Calculation Modules in Gas / Water / Heating / Cooling

What each module does, where it’s used, and why it matters for network design, contingency analysis, and planning.

  • Gas Basic Module / Contingency Analysis

    Model, design, and optimize gas networks. Handle routine & emergency conditions.

  • Water Basic Module / Contingency Analysis

    Design and simulate water distribution networks: pressure zones, supply/demand, and emergency flows.

  • Heating / Cooling Basic Module

    Plan thermal networks; simulate heating/cooling flows; analyze thermal losses and return behavior under load.

  • Time Simulation / Dynamic Demand Profiles

    Use synthetic or measured profiles for loads/supplies; analyze daily & seasonal variations over time.

  • Geographical Data & GIS Integration

    Import GIS/shapefiles; visualize networks over maps; use elevation data for map-based editing & display.

  • Technical Databases & Libraries

    Libraries for pipes, pumps, valves, heat exchangers; customize elements and update network data.

  • Pump / Valve / Heat Exchanger Modelling

    Models for flow, pressure drop, regulation, heat exchangers; simulate automatic control & regulation.

  • Fire Water Planning

    Simulate emergency fire-fighting water supply at hydrants; check pressure levels under fire loads.

  • Length / Elevation Profiles

    2D profiles for pipes: pressure vs elevation & flow; useful for visualization & diagnosing issues.

  • Reliability / Contingency & Investment Analysis

    Scenario planning: component failure impacts, pipeline criticality, and investment planning.

Why Choose NEPLAN Gas, Water & Heating / Cooling?

  • Plan non-electrical infrastructure with the same level of depth & rigor as electrical systems, using a single platform.
  • Understand and manage interdependencies between gas, water, and thermal networks and the electrical domain-for example, heating loads affecting electricity demand, or gas supply constraints affecting heating.
  • Improve reliability of supply under varying demand patterns, emergencies, or infrastructure changes.
  • Optimize operational costs (pumping, heat losses etc.) and investment decisions via scenario and variant management.
  • Visual tools (GIS, profiles, maps) improve clarity and communication across stakeholders.
